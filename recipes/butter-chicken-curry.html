Butter Chicken Curry

A beloved Indian classic — tender chicken thighs bathed in a velvety, spiced tomato and cream sauce with a deep golden color and irresistible aroma. Rich with butter, warm spices, and a gentle heat, this is comfort food at its most fragrant and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 800 grams boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 200 grams plain whole-milk yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon neutral oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 400 grams crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 200 milliliters heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Fresh cilantro and naan or basmati rice, to serve

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine 200 grams plain whole-milk yogurt, 2 tablespoons lemon juice, 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1 teaspoon ground cumin, 1 teaspoon ground turmeric, 1 teaspoon chili powder, and 1 teaspoon salt. Add 800 grams chicken thighs and toss to coat well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.
  2. Sear the chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon neutral oil in a large skillet or heavy-bottomed pan over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken in a single layer and cook until lightly charred on the outside, about 4–5 minutes per side. The chicken does not need to be fully cooked through. Set aside.
  3. Build the sauce base: In the same pan, melt 3 tablespoons unsalted butter over medium heat. Add 1 large yellow onion, finely diced and cook until deeply golden and softened, about 10 minutes. Add 4 garlic cloves, minced and 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated and cook for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the spices and tomatoes: Stir in 1 teaspoon ground coriander, 1 teaspoon garam masala, and 1 tablespoon tomato paste. Cook for 1 minute. Add 400 grams crushed tomatoes and stir to combine. Let the sauce simmer over medium-low heat for 15 minutes until thickened and the oil starts to separate slightly.
  5. Blend the sauce: Using an immersion blender (or carefully transferring to a countertop blender), blend the sauce until completely smooth. Return to the pan over medium-low heat.
  6. Add cream and chicken: Pour in 200 milliliters heavy cream and 1 teaspoon sugar. Stir to combine. Add the seared chicken back to the pan and simmer gently for 10–15 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked through and the sauce is rich and velvety.
  7. Taste and finish: Adjust seasoning with salt as needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot alongside warm naan or fluffy basmati rice.
