Classic Lasagna-with-bolognese-sauce

A timeless Italian comfort dish — layers of silky pasta, rich slow-cooked meat ragù, creamy béchamel, and golden melted cheese. This is the kind of lasagna that fills the kitchen with warmth and brings everyone to the table.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams lasagna sheets (dried)
  • 400 grams ground beef
  • 200 grams ground pork
  • 1 yellow onion, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, finely diced
  • 2 celery stalks, finely diced
  • 400 grams crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 120 milliliters dry red wine
  • 750 milliliters whole milk
  • 60 grams unsalted butter
  • 60 grams all-purpose flour
  • 0.3 teaspoons nutmeg (freshly grated)
  • 200 grams mozzarella, shredded
  • 80 grams Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoons salt and black pepper

Steps

  1. Build the soffritto: Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add 1 yellow onion, finely diced, 2 carrots, finely diced, and 2 celery stalks, finely diced. Cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and lightly golden. Add 3 garlic cloves, minced and cook for 1 more minute.
  2. Brown the meat: Add 400 grams ground beef and 200 grams ground pork to the pot. Break up the meat and cook over medium-high heat until browned all over. Season with 1 teaspoons salt and black pepper.
  3. Deglaze and add tomatoes: Pour in 120 milliliters dry red wine and let it simmer until mostly evaporated, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in 2 tablespoons tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes, then add 400 grams crushed San Marzano tomatoes. Stir everything together well.
  5. Simmer the Bolognese: Reduce heat to low, partially cover, and let the ragù simmer gently, stirring occasionally, until thick and deeply flavored.
  6. Make the béchamel: In a saucepan, melt 60 grams unsalted butter over medium heat. Whisk in 60 grams all-purpose flour and cook for 10 minutes.
  7. Gradually pour in 750 milliliters whole milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Cook until thickened, about 8 minutes. Season with 1 teaspoons salt and black pepper and 0.3 teaspoons nutmeg (freshly grated).
  8. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F). Lightly butter a 9×13 inch baking dish.
  9. Layer the lasagna: Spread a thin layer of béchamel on the bottom of the dish. Add a layer of 250 grams lasagna sheets (dried), then Bolognese, then béchamel, then a sprinkle of 200 grams mozzarella, shredded and 80 grams Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated. Repeat layers until ingredients are used up, finishing with béchamel and a generous topping of both cheeses.
  10. Bake covered: Cover tightly with foil and bake until the pasta is tender and the sauce is bubbling around the edges.
  11. Bake uncovered: Remove the foil and bake until the top is golden and bubbling.
  12. Rest before serving: Let the lasagna rest before slicing — this helps the layers hold together
