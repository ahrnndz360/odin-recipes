Couscous and Chickpeas with Feta Cheese

A bright and satisfying Mediterranean bowl — fluffy couscous tossed with golden-spiced chickpeas, crisp vegetables, fresh herbs, and topped with creamy crumbled feta. Light enough for a weeknight, bold enough to impress, and ready in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 300 grams couscous (instant)
  • 360 milliliters vegetable broth, hot
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 400 grams canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 0.5 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 0.5 teaspoons chili flakes (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt and black pepper
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 200 grams cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 0.5 red onion, finely sliced
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 30 grams fresh parsley, roughly chopped
  • 15 grams fresh mint, roughly chopped
  • 200 grams feta cheese, crumbled

Steps

  1. Cook the couscous: Place 300 grams couscous in a large heatproof bowl. Pour over 360 milliliters hot vegetable broth and 1 tablespoon olive oil. Stir briefly, cover tightly with a plate or plastic wrap, and let sit for 5 minutes.
  2. Fluff and cool: Uncover the couscous and fluff thoroughly with a fork, separating all the grains. Spread out slightly and allow to cool to room temperature.
  3. Crisp the chickpeas: Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add 400 grams chickpeas, 1 teaspoon ground cumin, 1 teaspoon smoked paprika, 0.5 teaspoons ground coriander, and 0.5 teaspoons chili flakes if using. Season with 1 teaspoon salt and black pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 6–8 minutes until the chickpeas are lightly crispy and golden.
  4. Prepare the vegetables: While the chickpeas cook, dice 1 red bell pepper, 1 cucumber, halve 200 grams cherry tomatoes, and finely slice 0.5 red onion. Add everything to the bowl of couscous.
  5. Dress the salad: Drizzle 3 tablespoons lemon juice and 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil over the couscous and vegetables. Toss well to combine. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper as needed.
  6. Add herbs and chickpeas: Fold in 30 grams fresh parsley and 15 grams fresh mint. Add the warm spiced chickpeas and toss gently so they stay slightly crispy.
  7. Finish with feta: Transfer to a serving dish or individual bowls. Top generously with 200 grams crumbled feta cheese. Serve immediately, or refrigerate and serve as a cold salad — it keeps well for up to 2 days.
